# Full Risk Assessment Report

## 1. Introduction

This report presents a structured risk assessment conducted to identify and evaluate potential risks within a simulated organizational environment.

## 2. Methodology

The assessment followed a standard risk management approach:

1. Risk Identification
2. Risk Analysis
3. Risk Evaluation
4. Risk Treatment

A qualitative model was used to assess:

- Likelihood (Low, Medium, High)
- Impact (Low, Medium, High)

Risk scores were determined using a simple matrix.

## 3. Risk Register (Sample)

| Risk ID | Risk Description               | Likelihood | Impact | Risk Score | Mitigation Strategy               | Owner              |
| ------- | ------------------------------ | ---------- | ------ | ---------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| R1      | Data breach due to weak access | High       | High   | Critical   | Implement MFA and access controls | IT Security Lead   |
| R2      | System downtime                | Medium     | High   | High       | Backup systems and redundancy     | IT Operations      |
| R3      | Regulatory non-compliance      | Low        | High   | Medium     | Regular compliance audits         | Compliance Officer |
| R4      | Third-party vendor failure     | Medium     | Medium | Medium     | Vendor risk assessment program    | Procurement        |

## 4. Key Findings

- High-impact risks are primarily related to information security and system availability
- Vendor and compliance risks require continuous monitoring
- Lack of strong controls increases exposure to critical risks

## 5. Recommendations

- Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Conduct regular risk reviews and audits
- Establish a vendor risk management framework
- Improve incident response planning

## 6. Conclusion

This risk assessment demonstrates the importance of structured risk management in identifying and mitigating organizational threats. Maintaining an up-to-date risk register is essential for effective decision-making and compliance.
